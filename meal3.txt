Omurice or omu-rice (オムライス, Omu-raisu) is an example of 
yōshoku (Western-influenced (Fusion cuisine) style of Japanese 
cuisine) consisting of an omelette made with fried rice
and thin, fried scrambled eggs, usually topped with ketchup.
It is a popular dish both commonly cooked at home and often 
found at western style diners in Japan.
